Aluminum

Aluminum

Aluminum is normally used by AFV's or light constructions, as it provides significantly more protection for a given weight. It is more costly and prone to spalling though.

CHA

Cast homogeneous Armor

Despite of being heavier than RHA, Cast steel material provides more resiliance against damage than its rolled counterpart. Highly vulnerable to spalling.

Ceramic

Ceramic

Ceramic is usually used as a material to ensure shells do not penetrate due to its high penetration resistance. Due to its frailty it is usually only used as a backing and is not meant to take the brunt of damage. Do not let it get penetrated or it will shatter.

DU

Depleted Uranium

Heavy yet extremely effective armor. Though costly, a slab of this can stop just about anything.
Has some nasty secondary effects when penetrated. More effective at higher thicknesses.

ERA

Explosive Reactive Armor

An explosive composite sandwiched between 2 plates. When penetrated the plate detonates damaging or even destroying the incoming shell degrading its performance. This material is heavy compared to RHA and unlike other materials, will damage anything near the detonation. Explosive rounds can make short work of this material.

RHA

Rolled Homogeneous Armor

Simple, generic, but trusty steel. The standard armor everything else is compared to.

Rubber

Rubber

Another material that while useless against kinetic rounds, excels at stopping shaped charges and spall

Textolite

Textolite

Fiberglass based material, this material provides decent protection agaisnt both chemical especially and kinetic rounds, while taking reduced damage from explosions.

Titanium

Titanium

Lightweight and super resiliant. But E X P E N S I V E. 60% Lighter than RHA for a given thickness.
Unlike aluminum works at high thicknesses but for a price.

NOTES

Layering armor can significantly improve protection against certain types of ammunition.
