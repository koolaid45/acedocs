How ACE Works — Quick Overview

ACE is a physics-based combat addon. Every shell is a real projectile simulated per-tick with gravity, drag, and collision detection. Penetration is computed from kinetic energy at impact, not from a fixed stat.

Key Concepts

Penetration is Calculated, Not Fixed

ACE doesn't use a stat sheet. When a round hits armour, the game computes penetration from the shell's kinetic energy at that moment, its mass, and the penetration area modifier. A heavier, faster shell penetrates more. At range, drag reduces velocity, reducing penetration.

Line of Sight vs Nominal Armour

Hitting armour at an angle increases effective thickness. A 100 mm plate hit at 60° presents ~200 mm of line-of-sight (LOS) armour. Each material has its own effectiveness multiplier on top of that.

Ductility

Each armour plate has a ductility setting (−1 to 1). Positive ductility reduces damage taken. Negative ductility (brittle) increases damage but also spall resistance trade-offs. Ceramic uses negative ductility to shatter effectively against kinetic rounds.

Guidance Systems

Wire-Guided (MCLOS/SACLOS)

Missile is connected to the launcher by a thin wire. Operator guides it manually or semi-automatically. Vulnerable to interference and requires line of sight throughout flight.

Infrared Seeker (IR)

Locks onto heat sources. Must acquire lock before or after launch. Susceptible to flares and IR jamming. Most common on AAMs and MANPADs.

Laser Guidance

Follows a laser spot painted on the target. Very accurate but requires continuous laser designation throughout the missile's flight.

Radar Homing

Homes in on radar emissions. Used by anti-radiation missiles (ARM). Cannot be spoofed by flares.

GPS

Navigates to a fixed coordinate. Used on guided bombs (GBU). Fire-and-forget but cannot track moving targets.

Inertial / Dumb

No guidance after launch. Travels in a straight line. No countermeasure vulnerability.

Armor Materials

Each material has unique properties for KE, HEAT, spall, and weight. Effectiveness multiplies the LOS armour value.

Penetration Probability (RHA formula)
penProb = clamp( 1 / (1 + exp(-43.94 × (maxPen / losArmor / eff - 1))), 0.0015, 0.9985 )
Logistic curve. At pen/armour = 1.0 → ~50% chance. Below 0.5 → near 0%. Above 1.5 → near 100%.
Breach (Overmatch) Probability
breachProb = clamp( (caliber / armor / eff - 1.3) / 5.7, 0, 1 )
When the caliber is ≥ 1.3× the effective armour thickness, there's a chance to punch clean through (overmatch). At 7× — guaranteed breach.

Engine Types

Petrol (GenericPetrol)

Gasoline engine. Highest power density per kg, highest RPM range. Burns petrol fuel only. Common in light vehicles and aircraft.

Diesel (GenericDiesel)

Diesel/Multifuel engine. Lower RPM ceiling, higher torque at low RPM. Fuel-efficient. Standard tank powerplant.

Electric

Electric motor. Peak torque from zero RPM. Very high RPM ceiling. Requires battery capacity. Silent running option.

Gas Turbine

Turbine engine. Extremely high power output. Multifuel. High idle RPM. Spool-up delay. Excellent power-to-weight ratio.

Radial

Aircraft-derived radial engine. High power density, petrol only. Used in WWII-era heavy vehicles and aircraft.

Wankel (Rotary)

Rotary piston engine. Very high RPM, compact, lightweight. Lower torque but very smooth power delivery.

Ballistics & Formulas

The actual formulas from ACE's source code — plus an interactive calculator.

Muzzle Velocity

ACF_MuzzleVelocity( PropMass, ProjMass )
PEnergy = PBase × ((1 + PropMass)^PScale − 1) Speed = (PEnergy × 2000 / ProjMass)^MVScale MuzzleVel = Speed [in m/s]
PBase = 4,000,000 · PScale = 0.6 · MVScale = 0.5
PropMass and ProjMass are in kilograms. More propellant = faster. Heavier shell = slower.
This is approximate — the game uses this simplified internal ballistic model.

Kinetic Energy & Penetration

ACF_Kinetic( Speed, Mass, LimitVel )
KE = (Mass × Speed²) / 2000 [kJ] Momentum = Speed × Mass fullKE = (Mass × Speed^1.8) / 2000 + Momentum overspeed = max(Speed − LimitVel, 0) Penetration = max(fullKE − (overspeed² / LimitVel×5) × (fullKE/200)^0.95, fullKE × 0.1)
Speed is in m/s (converted from game units via ÷ 39.37). LimitVel is the optimal penetration speed — AP uses 750 m/s, HE uses 100 m/s. Above LimitVel, efficiency drops.
Max Penetration (mm RHAe)
FrArea = π × (Caliber / 2)² [cm²] PenArea = FrArea^0.6 MaxPen = (Penetration / PenArea) × KEtoRHA KEtoRHA = 0.04 [conversion constant]
PenArea uses a 0.6 exponent — larger calibres have proportionally larger impact area but don't scale linearly. KEtoRHA = 0.04 converts the internal energy unit to millimetres of RHA.

Drag (In-Flight Velocity Loss)

Per-Tick Drag
FlightSpeed = Bullet.Flight:Length() [game units/s] Drag = (DragCoef × FlightSpeed²) / DragDiv Flight = Flight − Drag × direction × DeltaTime DragDiv = 8000 DragCoef = (FrArea / 10000) / ProjMass [AP/HE formula]
Drag is computed every game tick. Heavier shells lose less speed per tick. Larger cross-section (bigger calibre) = more drag. DragCoef is set per round-type and per shell configuration.

HE Blast

HE Explosion Parameters
BlastRadius = FillerMass^0.33 × 8 [metres] BlastEnergy = FillerMass × HEPower [kJ] (HEPower = 3000) MaxBlastPen = FillerMass × HEPower / HEBlastPenetration Fragments = floor((FillerMass / FragMass) × 500) FragVel = sqrt(FillerMass × 3000000 / FragMass / Fragments)
FillerMass is in kg. BlastRadius follows a cube-root scaling. More filler = bigger blast, but weight rises fast. Fragment count and velocity both depend on filler-to-casing ratio.

Ricochet

Ricochet Probability
RicoAngle = min(BaseRico − ((Speed − 800) / 39.37 / 5), 89) None = RicoAngle − 10 → 0% chance Mean = RicoAngle → 50% chance Max = RicoAngle + 10 → 100% chance AP BaseRico = 53° | HE = 70° | APFSDS = 30°
Higher-velocity shells ricochet at smaller angles. AP rounds ricochet more easily than HE. APFSDS punches through at almost any angle.
